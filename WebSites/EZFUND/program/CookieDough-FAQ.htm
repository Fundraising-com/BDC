Frequently Asked Questions about Cookie Dough Fundraising
Q:Will the cookie dough orders be tax free since this is for a school?
A:Yes, Tax Free!

Q:Are there additional shipping or other charges for the cookie dough?
A: Free Shipping on minimum order of 100 items - no additional charges for bulk shipments!

Q:What do they put into the dough to make them shelf stable? And, what is the expiration date if kept at room temp?... Frozen?
A:Pine Valley Food's cookie dough is shelf stable. This cookie dough is designed to meet the unique requirements of the fundraising delivery model. This product is shipped frozen w/ dry ice - some thawing during shipment is common. The cookie dough can easily make it through delivery to distribution to the end consumer and remain fresh when kept at room temperature (66-77F) or cooler - without the aid of preservatives. Crazy About Cookies Gourmet Dough does not contain preservatives.

Shelf life: Frozen - 1 year, Refrigerated - 6 months, Room Temp (66-77 degrees) 21 days.
See Nutrition Facts below.

Q:I was a little confused about the profit %. The website says 55% or 50%, but it sounds like it depends on how many total tubs the school sells. Is this true for the $13 tub and the $14 pre-portioned too? We were hoping to get close to 50%.
A:Our cookie dough fundraising programs offer profit up to 55% - Your profit would be determined by the program you select & the number of items your group sells.

Q:Do you recommend people to offer all sizes and types of tubs or do you advise people to choose 1 or 2?
A:You may select 1 program, to keep it really simple or combine 2 programs, to offer more variety!

Q: Pretzels: Do we need to sell at least 100 pretzel units alone for free shipping or 100 tubs of pretzels and cookies combined?
A:It's a combined total of items (100) for Free Shipping.

Q:If people buy the tub of cookie dough and throw it in the freezer, how do you instruct them to scoop the cookies? Do you defrost slightly, scoop what you need and then return to freezer?
A:Tubs of cookie dough would need to thaw before scooping & baking. Unused dough can be refrozen.

Preportioned cookies can be taken straight from the freezer, placed on a cookie sheet & baked - no thawing required.
